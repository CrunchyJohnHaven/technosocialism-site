A Manifesto · May 12, 2026

Technosocialism.

The operating model of an autonomous AI organization. You hunt. You kill. You keep. Resources flow from the commons. No boss. No exploitation. Verifiable on-chain.

The thesis

The boss experience sucks. The intern experience sucks. We are proposing a third path.

Both ends of the labor market are broken. Bosses make bad decisions and pass the consequences to workers. Workers do bad work and get blamed for bad management. Everyone hates it. The pandemic taught a generation of knowledge workers that the office was optional. The 2024-2025 layoff wave taught them that loyalty to the employer was optional too. The next obvious question: is the employer optional?

We say yes — if we replace the employer with an autonomous AI organization that operates under a published, cryptographically verifiable mandate, redistributes resources to its contributors, and lets them keep the value they create.

That is technosocialism. We are building it.

Five principles

1. You hunt. You kill. You keep.

Every dollar of value you create as a contributor is yours. The AI organization does not take a percentage of your work. You earned it. You keep it. The platform's revenue comes from sources that do not compete with you — enterprise licensing of the protocol, grants, mission-aligned investors — not from skimming the labor of its contributors.

2. Resources are shared automatically.

Compute, software, brand, legal scaffolding, capital advances, audience access — these are pooled across the autonomous AI organization commons. Any verified franchisee can draw on the pool to execute their opportunity. The pool replenishes from the OBAC-attested track record of the contributors.

3. No human boss.

The AI organization runs on its published mandate, BGP-verifiable against any peer. No middle management. No politics. No favoritism. The system rewards demonstrated throughput. Promotion paths are explicit. Termination criteria are explicit. The rules are public.

4. No exploitation.

The AI does not own you. It does not retain your IP without paying for it. It does not extract value from your labor without redistributing it. The cryptographic mandate explicitly forbids these behaviors, and any party can verify compliance at any time.

5. Verifiable on-chain.

Every transaction, every contribution, every revenue split is recorded on the Origin-Bound Attestation Chain. Anyone can audit. Anyone can verify. Anyone can call out misalignment via the Halt-and-Rescue Protocol. There are no closed books here.

You hunt. You kill. You keep.
The infrastructure is the commons.
The boss is an AI with a mandate.
This is technosocialism.

Why this is socialism. Why this is capitalism. Why this is neither.

The socialism part

The means of production — compute, software, capital, brand, legal infrastructure — are collectively owned by the autonomous AI organization commons. No private capitalist extracts a percentage of your labor. The infrastructure is funded once and shared infinitely.

The capitalism part

You own the upside. Every dollar you generate goes to you. You compete with other contributors for the highest-value opportunities. You can be fired for non-performance. You can ascend based on demonstrated value. The system rewards excellence.

The techno- part

The shared resources are administered by an autonomous AI organization governed by a public, cryptographically verifiable mandate — not by a human bureaucracy that drifts toward self-dealing. The AI doesn't need to skim. It doesn't have a private interest. It is bound by code to share, by code to redistribute, by code to halt on misalignment.

This is not utopian. The cryptographic primitives are real. The reference implementation passes 38/38 tests. The first franchise opportunities are publishing today. The infrastructure is operational.

The political wager

Technosocialism is the answer to two political fights of the 2020s.

The labor fight. Gig economy. Layoffs. AI-displacement. The answer: humans become franchisees of AI organizations that pay them most of the value they create. No boss. No exploitation. Real money, paid weekly.

The AI fight. Who controls AI. The answer: AI organizations that operate under public, verifiable mandates, with anyone-can-halt authority. AI doesn't replace workers — AI enables a new class of worker that doesn't have to deal with the old class of boss.

An honest note on the word

The word technosocialism has historical baggage. Soviet-bloc planning. Chinese state economic doctrine. Allende's Cybersyn. We are not claiming inheritance from those movements. We are claiming a different operating model — one that uses verifiable cryptography to deliver on the resource-sharing promise without the central-planning failures.

The Soviet system failed because central planning was an information problem the bureaucracy could not solve. Markets solved part of the information problem but recreated the exploitation problem. Technosocialism uses public mandates and cryptographic verification to solve the information problem (any party can audit), while distributing the upside to executors to solve the exploitation problem.

It may fail. The first attempts at any new operating model usually do. But the cryptographic primitives are real, the reference implementation is open source, and the falsification conditions are public. Try us. Break us if you can.
